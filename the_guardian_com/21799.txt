Four men have been charged with the murder of postmaster's son Craig Hodson-Walker during an armed raid on a post office on 9 January, police said today.Christopher Morrissey, 31, Declan Morrissey, 33, Anselm Ribera, 33, and Adrian Snape, 24, have also been charged with the attempted murder of Hodson-Walker's father, Ken.The men were arrested on Tuesday morning in a series of dawn raids across south Birmingham, police said.Detective superintendent Jon Groves said high-visibility patrols around post offices and general stores in the area would continue. Thanking the public and 150 police workers and staff for their part in the investigation, Groves said: "Our thoughts remain with the Hodson-Walker family at this sad time."The men will appear at Redditch magistrates court tomorrow.
