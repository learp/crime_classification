Police tonight charged Mark Hobson with the murders of his girlfriend, her twin sister and an elderly couple.Mr Hobson will appear at Harrogate magistrates court tomorrow morning amid tight security, North Yorkshire police said. Police arrested the 34-year-old former dustman on Sunday after a week-long manhunt involving hundreds of officers, prompted by the discovery of the four bodies at two different addresses on the same day, July 18. The bodies of twins Claire and Diane Sanderson, 27, were found at Hobson's flat in the village of Camblesforth, near Selby. The bodies of an elderly couple, James Britton, 80, and his wife Joan, 82, were found 25 miles away in Strensall and police linked the inquiries. Mr Hobson, a father of one, was escorted to Harrogate police station for questioning on Tuesday, having been treated in hospital for dehydration and minor abrasions after his arrest on Sunday.He was arrested after being spotted buying matches, water and cigarette papers by elderly garage owner Derrick North. Mr North's garage, near the village of Shipton, is just a few miles from Strensall.
