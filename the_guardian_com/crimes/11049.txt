Police in Hampshire have launched a murder investigation after the death of a five-year-old boy, who sustained life-threatening injuries during a fire that engulfed a car.Tommy Sheldon was injured in the blaze in Merdon Castle Lane, Hursley, Hampshire, on 11 August but died on Monday.Hampshire Constabulary said the boy's mother, 37, and his six-year-old sister, who were also inside the Ford Fiesta, were still in hospital receiving treatment.No arrests have been made as a postmortem examination is scheduled to confirm the child's cause of death.Detectives said all options were being considered, including whether the fire was deliberately lit."The nature of the injuries suffered by all those involved, and the need to ensure their health and recovery takes priority, means that this will be a lengthy investigation spanning many weeks," Detective Inspector Simon Baker said.
