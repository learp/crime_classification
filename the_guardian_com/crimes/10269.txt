Five men and a woman have been arrested on suspicion of the commission, preparation or instigation of acts of terrorism, police said.The arrests in London were part of a planned intelligence-led operation by Scotland Yard's counterterrorism command along with armed officers, but were not linked to the Olympics, officers said.A 24-year-old man was shot with a stun gun during his arrest, but did not require hospital treatment, a force spokesman added. The six were taken to a south-east London police station.A 29-year-old man was arrested on a west London street, a 21-year-old man and the woman were detained at a separate residential premises in west London, and three other men, aged 18, 24, and 26, were held in east London."Searches under the Terrorism Act 2000 are being carried out at eight residential premises in east, west and north London and one business premises in east London," the spokesman said.
