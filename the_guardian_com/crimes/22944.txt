A nanny hired by Ann Summers chief executive Jacqueline Gold has admitted attempting to poison the businesswoman with screen wash.Allison Cox, 33, who looked after Gold's daughter Scarlett at her mansion in Whyteleafe, Surrey, contaminated food prepared by her chef in an attempt to get the other member of staff into trouble.She pleaded guilty at Guildford crown court to one charge of administering poison with intent to injure, aggrieve or annoy.Cox had been charged with trying to poison Gold with sugar and salt, but the indictment was amended. Sentencing is on 4 March.
