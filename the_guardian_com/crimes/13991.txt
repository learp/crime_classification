A man has been charged with the murders of two elderly women, police have said.Andrew Simon Flood is accused of killing Margaret Biddolph, 78, and Annie Leyland, 88, who were found dead in their homes in Southport, Merseyside. Postmortem examinations confirmed that both victims died of asphyxia.The 43-year-old, from Southport, will appear at South Sefton magistrates court in Bootle on Friday.Biddolph's body was discovered at her property in Lonsdale Road on Tuesday evening, and Leyland was found dead at her nearby Birch Street home in the early hours of Wednesday.A police spokeswoman said Flood was also charged with robbery in connection with an incident in Janes Brook Road on 18 February.The families of both victims issued short statements through Merseyside police on Thursday night.Leyland's son, Philip, said: "Firstly, I am co-operating with the police to help them carry out their duty and I would appeal to anyone with information to come forward."Secondly, I ask to be left alone to grieve quietly and to come to terms with what has happened."Alex Biddolph and Anne Pearson, the son and daughter of Biddolph, said: "We as a family would like to thank the members of the public who have assisted the police with their inquiries."We would also urge anyone with any information that could help the police with their investigation to get in touch."A neighbour described Biddolph as a quiet woman whom she would often see working in her garden.Marianne Lott, who lives opposite Biddolph's home, said: "She was very jolly and independent. She was in her 80s but that didn't stop her getting taxis out and about to get groceries or do some shopping."Lott said her neighbour had been a widow for 40 years and had three adult children.Biddolph is believed to have lived in her home for 36 years.A neighbour of Leyland said: "She was the sort of person who kept herself to herself."
