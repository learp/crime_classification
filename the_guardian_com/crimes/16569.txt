A 40-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of murder after three females were stabbed, one fatally, in south London, police said.The attacks took place at homes in Brixton on Monday night. The arrested man is understood to know all three of the victims.Police were called to an address in Redlands Way at 6.41pm, where a 46-year-old woman was found with a number of injuries including stab wounds. She is in a critical condition at a south London hospital.At 7.10pm officers were called to an address in nearby Tilson Gardens, following reports that two females had been attacked. A 28-year-old woman was pronounced dead at the scene at 7.31pm, while a 17-year-old girl with stab wounds was taken to hospital, where she is in a serious but stable condition.It is understood that the 17-year-old and the 28-year-old, but not the 46-year-old woman, are related.A manhunt was launched and the suspect was arrested at 12.42am on a bus in Leigham Court Road, Streatham, after traffic officers stopped the bus and boarded it.He is in custody at a south London police station. Detectives are not seeking anyone else in connection with the investigation.Speaking at Brixton police station on Tuesday afternoon, Det Insp Will Reynolds said the 17-year-old girl and the 28-year-old woman were sisters.He said: "The lady who was fatally stabbed is the sister of the 17-year-old girl who tried to protect her."The teenager suffered stab wounds to her head and hands.Young children were present at the scene of both attacks, which involved "extreme violence", and officers are trying to establish whether they saw anything significant.According to neighbours, the murdered woman had two children, a boy of 12 and a three-year-old girl.A witness said she saw the attack on the 46-year-old woman, which took place on a balcony of a Streatham housing estate flat. The neighbour said she heard someone screaming "Please no" and "Help me, help me it's murder", and allegedly saw the man holding the woman by the "scruff of the neck".
