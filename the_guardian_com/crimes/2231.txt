A man in his 50s has been shot by police after threatening a woman with a gun, a force spokeswoman said today.Firearms officers were called to an address in Copthorne, West Sussex, at 10pm yesterday after receiving reports of an armed man threatening a woman inside.Officers negotiated with the pair, then at 10.55pm "a firearm was discharged by police" and the man received a hand injury, a Sussex police spokeswoman said."Officers gave him first aid before he was treated at the New East Surrey hospital in Redhill, where he is assisting police with their inquiries."A woman was led safely from the house. Firearms have been found in the house and their viability and type are yet to be ascertained."The spokeswoman said the Independent Police Complaints Commission had been notified.
