Five men were charged last night with the murder of a man in Sheerness, Kent, in April. Christopher Alaneme, 18, was stabbed to death on Sheerness high street; another man was wounded.The five charged are Peter Connolly, 29, a painter and decorator, Andrew Giblin, 26, a bank worker, Terence Beaney, 23, a plasterer, Sean Duhig, 23, and Gerry Duhig, 27, both plasterers.Messrs Connolly, Giblin and Beaney face a further charge of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm. All five are from south-east London and will appear before magistrates in Maidstone today.
