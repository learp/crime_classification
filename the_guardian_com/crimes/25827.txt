Seventy-four officers serving in the Metropolitan police received criminal records between 2000 and 2005, it emerged yesterday. Another 100 were also convicted in that time and have left the force, after resigning or being fired.The majority of the serving officers, 61, were convicted of drink driving, while the rest received a conviction or caution for minor assault or criminal damage.Any police officer convicted of a criminal offence has to disclose that to the force and faces misconduct proceedings.Scotland Yard released the figures after a request under the Freedom of Information Act.
