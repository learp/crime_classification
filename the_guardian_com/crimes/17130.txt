Former rocker Gary Glitter is to appeal against his conviction and three-year jail sentence for committing obscene acts with two Vietnamese girls.His lawyer, Le Thanh Kinh, said yesterday that Glitter, 61, whose real name is Paul Gadd, maintained that the evidence given by a victim and a witness at his trial in the southern resort of Vung Tau this month was "unreliable" and "unreasonable"."She knew the truth but never complained until it all came out in the newspapers," Mr Kinh alleged of the 11-year-old girl, identified as Ng.The appeal should be heard in three months.
