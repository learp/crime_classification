Police digging up the garden of a convicted paedophile said yesterday no human remains were found. The search of the former home of paedophile Leslie Ford-Thrussell in Croydon, south London, had ended, a police spokesman confirmed. It had been alleged the bodies of children were buried 35 years ago in the back garden of the semi-detached house.The spokesman said: "We are now reviewing the situation as a result of information gathered during the investigation." Ford-Thrussell was jailed in 2004 on seven counts of rape and 23 of indecent assault.
