A 30-year-old man is expected to appear in court on Friday charged with the murder of three men who were hit by a car while protecting shops and homes from looters in Birmingham during the riots in the early hours of 10 August.Three men, aged 17, 23 and 26, have already appeared in court charged with their murder after the incident in the Winson Green area of Birmingham.West Midlands police said that the fourth man was charged in connection with the deaths of Haroon Jahan, 21, and brothers Shazad Ali, 30, and Abdul Musavir, 31.On Monday, Joshua Donald, 26, Adam King, 23, and a 17-year-old who cannot be named were remanded in custody at Birmingham crown court to appear at the same court for a plea and case management hearing on 9 December.Donald, of Kelsall Croft, Ladywood, and the youth, from Edgbaston, appeared by videolink. King, of no fixed address, was in the dock.The victims' bodies were released for burial on Wednesday by a coroner.Police said the bodies had been released to their grieving families by Birmingham and Solihull coroner Aidan Cotter.An open-air prayer event has been organised in Summerfield Park in the Winson Green area for Thursday. The service, expected to last about 30 minutes, will be followed by private burials.A police spokeswoman said: "There will be a visible presence of police officers at the event, but not high numbers as this is a peaceful event which is being supported by the police."
