Police investigating the deaths of three heroin users in Lancashire within hours of each other have made 10 arrests.Detectives said a strong batch being sold on the streets of north-east Lancashire may have caused the deaths.Robin Ferguson, 53, Emlyn Hodgkinson, 36, and Zaheer Chaudrey, 32, were found at separate addresses in Burnley, Nelson and Colne.Robin Ferguson was found at a house in Tennis Street, Burnley, at 11.15pm on Friday and 20 minutes later the body of Emlyn Hodgkinson was discovered at Oak Street, Colne.At about 12.20am on Saturday the body of Zaheer Chaudrey was discovered at a house in Eagle Street, Nelson.Detective Chief Inspector Stuart Dixon of Lancashire police said: "From our inquiries today we believe that there may be a strong batch of heroin in the area and I would urge anyone who uses this drug to contact their local drug support agency."There could be an increased risk in taking stronger heroin and users should seek help and advice if concerned."At this current stage we are treating the deaths of these men as unexplained."A 41-year-old man and 38-year-old woman from Burnley, a 32-year-old man from Brierfield, a 34-year-old woman from Colne and a 28-year-old woman from Nelson were arrested on suspicion of being concerned in the supply of heroin.A 29-year-old man and 43-year-old woman, both from Colne, were arrested on suspicion of supplying an article to administer drugs.A 51-year-old Accrington man and a 40-year-old Burnley woman were arrested on suspicion of possessing a class A drug and a 40-year-old woman from Burnley was arrested on suspicion of possessing a class B drug.All have been bailed until 27 May pending further inquiries.
