A live shark was stolen from a garden aquarium in Hampshire over the weekend.The rare marble shark - one half of the only breeding pair in the UK - was taken from a brick outbuilding in Farnborough on July 26.The 2ft-long mottled brown female's owner, Peter Newman, 68, found the door of the aquarium open and all the lights on. The shark was missing.Stephen Court, a Hampshire police constable, said the shark may have been targeted by thieves due to its rarity."The victim is desperate for the fish to be returned and we urge anyone with information about the burglary to contact us," he said."Due to the unusual type of shark it will be difficult to sell in this country."
