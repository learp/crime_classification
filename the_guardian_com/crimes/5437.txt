A man was arrested today on suspicion of murdering an Oxfordshire woman more than 14 years ago, police said.Vikki Thompson, 30, was attacked while walking her dog in the village of Ascott-under-Wychwood on 12 August 1995. She was hit over the head with a rock and dragged across a field before being dumped near the Oxford to Worcester railway line, where she was attacked again.The mother-of-two was discovered by her husband and friends after the couple's collie returned home alone. She died at the John Radcliffe hospital in Oxford six days later.Officers from Thames Valley police's major crime review team confirmed they had arrested a 34-year-old man on suspicion of murder. The man, from Ascott-under-Wychwood, remains in police custody in Oxfordshire. He has not been charged.Police investigating Thompson's killing in 1995 said there was no evidence to suggest either sex or robbery was a motive.• This article was amended on Thursday 22 October 2009 for legal reasons.
