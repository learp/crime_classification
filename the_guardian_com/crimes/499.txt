Police have arrested a man after discovering two suspected explosive devices in a case believed to be linked to animal rights activism.Both devices were found in Caversham, Oxfordshire, Thames Valley police said, adding that the incident was unrelated to terrorism.Police sources said officers were "looking along the lines of animal rights" in connection with the discovery. Army bomb disposal experts were today working on disabling the second device, which was found this morning.The first device was found and made safe last night.Police said officers were called to a house at 11pm yesterday after the owner reported suspicious activity outside.They discovered the first device in the driveway and later arrested a 39-year-old man in the nearby area.The man remains in custody, a police spokesman said.The second device was discovered this morning after a police search of the area.It was understood both devices were experimental and did not appear to be the work of professional explosives experts.
