An acclaimed dramatisation of the Stephen Lawrence murder inquiry was among the winners yesterday at an award ceremony honouring the best in live entertainment.The production, The Colour of Justice, written by Guardian journalist Richard Norton-Taylor and produced at the Tricycle Theatre, London, took the outstanding achievement award for theatre at the Time Out Live awards in London. Other winners included Geordie comedian Ross Noble who took the title for best stand-up comic. BBC radio station GLR, which faces a shake-up in its output to include a greater emphasis on speech, was rewarded for its services to live music. Meltdown 99, the music festival curated by cult musician Nick Cave and which included performances from Kylie Minogue and Barry Humphries, was named best live music event. The Time Out Live awards, now in their 14th year, took place at the Groucho Club in Soho.
