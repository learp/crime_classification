A plea on behalf of a 19-year-old Liverpool football fan serving a 15-year sentence in a Bulgarian jail for attempted murder is to be made in the Commons today.Louise Ellman, the Labour MP for Liverpool Riverside, is to mount a debate on the case of Michael Shields of Wavertree, who says he was in bed in his hotel when a barman, Martin Georgiev, was attacked with a rock during a street brawl in the Bulgarian resort of Varna.Another man, Graham Sankey, an electrician from Anfield, has reportedly confessed to the crime, but the Bulgarian courts have so far refused to accept this as evidence.
