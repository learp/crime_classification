The prime suspect in the murder of 11-year-old Rhys Jones was in custody yesterday after a series of early-morning police raids.Merseyside officers arrested 12 people during the raids on 10 addresses in the Croxteth area of Liverpool, searching for an apparently hooded gunman who shot the schoolboy as he walked home from football practice last August.Four of those arrested, two boys aged 16 and 17 and two men aged 24 and 25, were being questioned on suspicion of murder at separate police stations. It is the third time officers have arrested the 17-year-old, who was held previously on suspicion of murder and firearms offences. More than 100 officers were involved in the raids, including a convoy of police vehicles which blocked off the surrounding streets in case any of the suspects attempted to escape. Forensic science teams and scene-of-crime officers began searching each of the homes yesterday. Merseyside police said they were working towards building strong cases against the suspects. The chief constable, Bernard Hogan-Howe, said yesterday: "A lot of painstaking work has gone into this investigation, which continues. The public and Rhys's parents have been very supportive throughout and we are grateful for their confidence in the investigation."A 16-year-old boy was arrested on suspicion of assisting an offender, attempting to pervert the course of justice and possessing a gun and ammunition. A man, 49, and boy, 15, were held on suspicion of assisting an offender and attempting to pervert the course of justice. Three women, aged 50, 54 and 21, and a 22-year-old man, were held on suspicion of attempting to pervert the course of justice. A 17-year-old boy was held on suspicion of assisting an offender.Rhys was walking across Fir Tree Drive South when he was shot. He is thought to have been caught in crossfire in a feud between rival gangs.
