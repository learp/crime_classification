A 62-year-old man has been charged with the murder of taxi driver whose body was found in his taxi near a railway station near Matlock a fortnight ago, Derbyshire police said. The man, who has not been named, will appear before magistrates in Derby accused of killing Stuart Ludlam, 43. The body of the taxi driver, who had been shot with a firearm, was discovered in the boot of his vehicle near Cromford station on September 17. Ludlam, from Darley Dale, was married with three children aged two, 12 and 15, and worked for MJ Taxis at Middleton-by-Wirksworth.
