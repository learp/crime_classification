A motorist whose dangerous driving caused the deaths of two sisters and their mother's partner was jailed for eight years yesterday.Andrew Winter, 32, of Crawley, Sussex, was driving at more than 90mph on a dual carriageway in Crawley when his car struck the central reservation, shot into the air and hit a Land Rover Freelander, Lewes crown court heard.Rebecca and Lucy Hassell, 15 and 12, died, as did Theodorius Lubbe, 44. The girls' mother, Amanda Hassell, and their sister Kim, 17, survived.Winter, who admitted three counts of death by dangerous driving, had minor injuries.
