Police are looking for a boy and a girl caught on close circuit television leaving a library with murdered 10-year-old Damilola Taylor shortly before his death.Detective Superintendent David Dillnut said the two may be able to give the police vital information about the last hours of Damilola's life. They want to know what the group talked about in case Damilola said anything that may lead to the identification of his murderers. His mother, Gloria, has said that he was being bullied in the days before his murder. Investigators have found a second knife in a carpark about 200m from the spot where the Nigerian boy bled to death in a stairwell on the North Peckham estate in south east London. Forensic officers are examining the knives. A witness has also described seeing three youths marching a younger boy off in a headlock on the day Damilola was killed.However, police said the group was walking away from the area where Damilola was believed to have been injured at 5pm that eveningDS Dillnut said Damilola was wearing a distinctive three-quarter length silver puffa jacket and appealed for more witnesses to come forward.Damilola was stabbed in the leg by a group of 11 to 14-year-olds on his way home from school on Monday.
