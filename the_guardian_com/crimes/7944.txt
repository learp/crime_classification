Merseyside police today arrested a 20-year-old man on suspicion of murdering the schoolboy Rhys Jones in Liverpool last year.The latest arrest takes the number of people being questioned on suspicion of killing the 11-year-old to five.Twelve people – nine men and three women - were arrested during dawn raids yesterday.The raids - involving more than 100 officers – were carried out in the Croxteth area of the city, where Rhys was shot in August last year.Of the 12 people detained, two boys, aged 16 and 17, and two men, aged 24 and 25, are being questioned on suspicion of murder at separate police stations. It is the third time officers have arrested the 17-year-old, who was held previously on suspicion of murder and firearms offences.Detectives had been given a further 12 hours, until 7pm this evening, to question the remaining suspects in connection with the killing.A 22-year-old male, who was arrested on suspicion of perverting the course of justice, was released on police bail late last night.A 16-year-old boy arrested on suspicion of assisting an offender, attempting to pervert the course of justice and possessing a gun and ammunition, is among the other suspects. A 49-year-old man and a 15-year-old boy are being held on suspicion of assisting an offender and attempting to pervert the course of justice, and a 17-year-old boy is being held on suspicion of assisting an offender.Three women, aged 21, 50 and 54, are being held on suspicion of attempting to pervert the course of justice.Rhys was shot in the neck as he walked home across Fir Tree Drive South after football practice. He is believed to have been caught in crossfire in a feud between rival gangs.
