Seven paintings by the London East End gangsters Ronnie and Reggie Kray sold at auction today for a total of £3,335.The paintings – four by Reggie and three by Ronnie, including self-portraits – had been expected to fetch between £200 and £300 when they went under the hammer in Lincoln. One, showing three men escaping from an island prison on a boat, sold for £675, while two more fetched £500 each, a spokeswoman for the auctioneers, Thomas Mawer and Son, said. A painting of the Krays by the serial poisoner Graham Young, made when all three attended prison art classes in the 1970s, did not sell.
