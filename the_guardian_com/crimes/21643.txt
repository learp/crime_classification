Two men have been arrested after the headless body of a man in his 20s was discovered, on fire, in Stockport.The man's body was discovered in Wellington Street, near the town centre, at 5.10am on Thursday.Greater Manchester police said they do not believe it is a gang-related killing. Two men, aged 29 and 31, have been arrested on suspicion of murder. The victim has not yet been identified.The Manchester Evening News reported that they had previously been arrested on other matters an hour before the man's body was found.Superintendent Pete Matthews from Greater Manchester police told reporters that detectives did not believe the death was related to criminal gangs.The crime scene close to Stockport Magistrates' Court has been cordoned off and a post-mortem examination will be carried out later.Fire crews were called to a fire in a wooded area and the decapitated body was discovered after the fire was put out.
