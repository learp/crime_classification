A 49-year-old man has been remanded in custody charged with the kidnap and sexual assault of a 10-year-old boy who vanished on a trip to local shops.Michael Jackson, from Oldbury, West Midlands, did not speak during the brief hearing at Warley magistrates court.Dressed in khaki green trousers and a grey sweatshirt, he only nodded to confirm his name, date of birth and address.He was charged with one count each of kidnap, false imprisonment, threats to kill and sexual assault.West Midlands police launched an investigation after the child was reported missing by a worried family member at 9.15am on Sunday when he failed to return home.Officers and members of the public searched the area for more than two hours before the boy was found at around 11.30am.Police said that a 48-year-old man arrested in connection with the investigation had been released on police bail pending further inquiries. A 30-year-old man was released without charge.Jackson is also charged with one count of child abduction, in connection with a separate alleged incident involving an 11-year-old girl in September.Assistant Chief Constable Gareth Cann, of West Midlands police, said: "We appreciate the impact that this investigation has had on the local community and we thank them for their support. We will continue to update partners and the local community as this inquiry progresses."Jackson was remanded in custody to appear at Wolverhampton crown court on 8 February next year.
