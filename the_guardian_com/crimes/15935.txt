  Click on the article to read more of the proceedingsDr Crippen and Ethel Le Neve were arrested for the murder of Crippen's wife, Cora Turner, also known as Belle Elmore. They had left the country before remains were found in the cellar of the house on Hilldrop Crescent, and were recognised by the master of the ship they were sailing on, the ss Montrose. He telegraphed his suspicions to the police, making Crippen the first criminal to be caught by this form of communication.Crippen was convicted of murder and executed on 23 November. Ethel Le Neve was aquitted.
