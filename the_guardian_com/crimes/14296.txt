A 26-year-old man was jailed for 20 years yesterday for the manslaughter of PC Sharon Beshenivsky following a failed robbery at a Bradford travel agents.Hassan Razzaq of Forest Gate, east London, was sentenced at Leeds crown court after a trial in Newcastle last year that cleared him of murder, but found him guilty of manslaughter, robbery and firearms offences.Sentencing, Mr Justice Andrew Smith said: "You knew that loaded guns, both a highly dangerous machine gun and the fatal pistol, would not only be carried by the raiders but used to threaten those who got in the way." Razzaq is the fourth man to be jailed for the fatal shooting of the 38-year-old officer on November 18 2005. Three other men tried for the killing have already been jailed. Razzaq's case had been adjourned to allow for the completion of pre-sentencing reports.Newcastle crown court heard that the gang who carried out the bungled robbery, which netted little more than Â£5,000, were "absolutely ruthless". Muzzaker Imtiaz Shah, 25, and 20-year-old Yusuf Abdillh Jamma, of Birmingham, two key members of the gang, were told by Mr Justice Andrew Smith that they must both serve at least 35 years before being considered for parole.Razzaq's 25-year-old brother, Faisal, who lived at the same address, was also cleared of PC Beshenivsky's murder and convicted of her manslaughter, robbery and firearms offences.He was given a life sentence and told he must serve at least 11 years before being considered for parole.PC Beshenivsky was shot and killed on the day of her youngest daughter's fourth birthday after responding to reports of a robbery in Bradford, West Yorkshire.She was the first woman police officer to be shot and killed on duty since the murder of Yvonne Fletcher outside the Libyan embassy in London in 1984. Beshenivsky's colleague, PC Teresa Milburn, was also shot in the street as the robbers escaped.
