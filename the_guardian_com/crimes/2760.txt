A woman who admitted murdering three men will stand by her guilty pleas.The bodies of Lukasz Slaboszewski, 31, Kevin Lee, 48, and John Chapman, 56, were found in ditches in Cambridgeshire in March.Joanna Dennehy, 31, of Peterborough, pleaded guilty to the murders at the Old Bailey last week but lawyers were granted time to apply to change these pleas after the court heard they were unexpected.They have confirmed to the court that no such application will be made.After entering her pleas, Dennehy told the judge, Mr Justice Sweeney: "I've pleaded guilty, and that's that."Her barrister, Nigel Lickley QC, said: "The course of the arraignment is not one we had anticipated."The bodies of the victims were found in March and April. All three had been stabbed.Dennehy also admitted preventing the lawful burial of all three murder victims and two charges of attempting to murder two other men.Gary Richards, 47, also known as Gary Stretch, will stand trial after denying three charges of preventing the lawful burial of the bodies of Slaboszewski, Lee and Chapman. He also denied attempting to murder the two other men.Leslie Layton, 36, pleaded not guilty to perverting the course of justice at an earlier hearing, and Robert Moore, 55, denied assisting an offender. All three accused are from Peterborough.
