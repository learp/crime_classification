A siege involving a man holding a six-year-old girl prisoner entered its third day today.Firearms officers continued to surround a house in the North Yorkshire resort of Scarborough, where the man has barricaded himself in.Police were called to the property, in Herdborough Road, on Monday night in response to reports of a domestic disturbance. It was reported that the man telephoned a TV station claiming to have stabbed the girl's mother.Police were unable to confirm that the man, who it is thought is aged in his 30s, was armed with a knife. Police negotiators were trying to encourage the man to surrender.Last night, the man could be heard shouting to police officers in the garden. At one point, he was heard to say: "I'm going to die tonight."A police spokesman said: "Owing to a belief that there was a potential threat to life, the firearms support unit was called to assist with the control of the incident. Police negotiators are also at the scene in a bid to bring the incident to a safe conclusion."The police intention remains to resolve the incident without harm to anybody involved. These efforts are still ongoing."
