A man was arrested in Manchester yesterday on suspicion of the murder of a 23-year-old man knocked down by a car following a 'racial incident'.David Lees had stopped at a garage with a group of friends in the Prestwich area early yesterday morning. They became involved in a row with a group of Asians. Police said the row escalated and Mr Lees was run over.The driver of the car drove off and Mr Lees died later at North Manchester General Hospital.The 25-year-old suspect was arrested yesterday afternoon and is being questioned by police.Superintendent Mark Robinson of North Manchester Police said: 'The disturbance involved up to about 20 people and is believed to have been between a white and Asian group.The incident has a racial element to it.'
