The grandmother of missing schoolgirl Tia Sharp will face no further action from police in connection with the child's death.Christine Sharp was arrested on 10 August this year, on suspicion of murder, after the 12-year-old's body was found in the loft of her home in New Addington, south London.Police confirmed yesterday that the 46-year-old had been released with no further action.Sharp's neighbour Paul Meehan remains on police bail.The 39-year-old, who was arrested on suspicion of assisting an offender, is bailed to return in mid-December.Stuart Hazell, the partner of Tia's grandmother, has been charged with her murder.The 37-year-old of New Addington, will stand trial next May. The Merton schoolgirl's body was found a week after she was reported missing.
