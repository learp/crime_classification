A man aged in his 80s is being interviewed by detectives investigating the Jimmy Savile abuse scandal, Scotland Yard has said.The man, from Berkshire, is being questioned after attending a south London police station by appointment on Thursday. He is being treated as part of the investigation that does not directly relate to Savile, the Metropolitan police said.Four arrests had been made previously as part of the national investigation into alleged sexual offences by Savile and others. Gary Glitter, Freddie Starr, DJ Dave Lee Travis and a man in his 70s, reported to be the former television producer Wilfred De'Ath, have been arrested and bailed as part of the inquiry.Scotland Yard is leading the investigation and has said officers are dealing with about 450 potential victims, the vast majority of whom say they fell prey to Savile.
