A man has been arrested on suspicion of murder after a teenager was stabbed to death on Thursday in Hastings, East Sussex.Paramedics fought to save the life of the 17-year-old at the scene.A Sussex police spokesman said: "Paramedics fought to save the boy who had been stabbed in the chest, but sadly he died."The emergency services were called to the scene close to The Shah pub at around 3.20pm. It is close to Ore railway station on the outskirts of the town.Sussex Police said the 31-year-old suspect from Rye was being held in police custody and said the stabbing did not appear to be a random attack.Police also appealed for anyone with information about the stabbing to come forward.Superintendent Trevor Botting, head of crime and operations for East Sussex, said: "This is a tragic incident and one that has shocked the local community. Our thoughts are with the victim's family, who understandably are devastated by the events of this afternoon."It is too early to speculate as to the circumstances of this tragedy, although early indications are that this was not a random attack."
