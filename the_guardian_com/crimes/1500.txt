A man has been charged in connection with the death of a rambler who was killed by a bull, police said.Paul Geoffrey Waterfall, 39, of Stanford on Soar, Leicestershire, has been charged with manslaughter by gross negligence.Roger Freeman, 63, was struck by a bull in November 2010 while walking with his wife along a public footpath through a field on Underhill farm, which was run by Waterfall. Freeman, from Glen Parva in Leicestershire, died at the scene from multiple injuries, Nottinghamshire police said.His wife, who was 67 at the time, was critically injured and spent three weeks in hospital. Police said she was continuing to recuperate at home.Alison Norton, the specialist prosecutor handling special crime at the Crown Prosecution Service, said: "I have concluded there is sufficient evidence and it is in the public interest to charge Paul Waterfall, who was responsible for Underhill farm and its livestock at the time of the incident, with gross negligence manslaughter."I would like to extend my deepest sympathies to the family of Roger Freeman."Waterfall has been bailed to appear at Nottingham magistrates court on 21 August.
