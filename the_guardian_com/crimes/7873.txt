A woman appeared in court today over the death of a man after an apparent row over queue-jumping at a supermarket.Antoinette Richardson, 37, of Sedgehill Road, Catford, south-east London, appeared at Sutton magistrates court charged with the murder of Kevin Tripp. The 57-year-old suffered serious head injuries after allegedly being assaulted at a branch of Sainsbury's in Merton, south-west London, on Tuesday evening. Tripp died in St George's hospital, in Tooting, the following night. Richardson was remanded in custody until September 18, when she will appear at the Old Bailey.Tony Virasami, 37, also from Catford, appeared at Wimbledon magistrates court yesterday charged with Tripp's murder. He was remanded in custody and will also appear at the Old Bailey on September 18.Tripp lived in Boundary Road, Merton, with his partner, Josephine, and a daughter. Breda McDonagh, 70, a neighbour and the mother of the MP for Mitcham and Morden, Siobhain McDonagh, said: "He was a very nice chap, a sociable man, cooperative, who would do anything he could for you."
