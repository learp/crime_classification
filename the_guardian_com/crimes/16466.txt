The Blackburn striker El Hadji Diouf will appear in court next month charged with driving offences, the Crown Prosecution Service said today.The 29-year-old was summonsed over allegations that he drove without a licence or insurance when stopped by police in Manchester in September.Diouf was stopped in a white Porsche Cayenne in Altrincham Road on 14 September. He is due to appear at Manchester City magistrates court on 10 May, a CPS spokesman said.The Senegalese striker has had a mixed season for Rovers. He scored the equaliser in Blackburn's 1-1 draw with Chelsea at Ewood Park last month but has found the net on only two other occasions this season. Blackburn next face Manchester United at home on Sunday.
