Three men accused of killing 29 mute swans in Bedfordshire last year appeared in court today.Stephen Barrett, 38, Marcus Walker, 51, and his 47-year-old brother, Darren, all of Chesham, Buckinghamshire, appeared before magistrates in Bedford, charged with the intentional killing of swans contrary to the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981.The birds, which had been shot, were found in October. They had been buried in a pit 10ft from the water at Radwell Lake. The lake, which was purpose built for legal shoots, is owned by the Walker brothers. At certain times of the year, it is legal to shoot waterfowl.Barrett was also accused of being in possession of items capable of committing the offence and a firearms breach.No pleas were taken, and the case was adjourned until March 13.
