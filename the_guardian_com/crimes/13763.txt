Four people have been arrested in connection with the murder of a 57-year-old man who was attacked by a gang in Leeds.The victim, who has not been named, died in hospital on Sunday following an incident in the Thorpe area of the city on Saturday, West Yorkshire police said.A spokesman said a 50-year-old man has been arrested along with a 37-year-old woman and two 16-year-old boys.The arrests were made in the Barnsley and Doncaster areas of South Yorkshire."Each remain in custody and inquiries are ongoing," the spokesman said.On Sunday, the force said reports were received at 7.23pm on Saturday of a disturbance at a bungalow in Middleton Lane, Thorpe, a village on the southern outskirts of LeedsIt said a man had been seriously assaulted by a number of men who arrived in a vehicle.Photographs showed a 4X4 vehicle was used to plough down a wall outside the bungalow and every window was smashed.The injured man was taken to Pinderfields hospital in Wakefield before being transferred to Leeds General infirmary for treatment to head injuries. He died in the early hours of Sunday morning.
