The comedian who threw a foam pie at Rupert Murdoch has had his jail sentence reduced by a third on appeal.Jonathan May-Bowles, 26, pleaded guilty last week to assaulting the 80-year-old media tycoon as he gave evidence to the House of Commons culture, media and sport committee about the phone-hacking scandal.May-Bowles, whose stage name is Jonnie Marbles, disrupted the proceedings by flinging a paper plate loaded with shaving foam at Murdoch.May-Bowles failed in an attempt to have his six-week prison sentence overturned on Friday at Southwark crown court in London, but it was reduced to four weeks.
