An off-duty police officer was yesterday being questioned about the shooting of her partner, a uniformed officer, inside the grounds of a Northern Ireland police station.The shooting occurred at around 7.15pm on Saturday at Newtownabbey station on the northern fringes of Belfast. Two shots were fired and both struck the officer. No one else was injured.The man was taken to hospital where his condition was yesterday described as serious but stable. The shooting has been referred to the Northern Ireland police ombudsman office. It will carry out an investigation.
