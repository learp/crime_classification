A 24-year-old man has been charged with a number of terrorism offences.Muhammad Aftab Suleman, of Crumpsall, Manchester, is accused of five offences under section 58 of the Terrorism Act 2000, Greater Manchester police said on Tuesday.The defendant will appear before Westminster magistrates court on Wednesday, police added.Officers from the north-west counter-terrorism unit searched an address in Peakdale Avenue, Crumpsall, on Friday.Suleman was arrested the following day after he reportedly attempted to board a flight at Manchester airport.Section 58 of the Terrorism Act relates to the collection of information which is likely to be useful to a person committing or preparing an act of terrorism.A police spokesman said: “There is believed to be no threat to the community of Greater Manchester relating to the circumstances of this investigation.”
