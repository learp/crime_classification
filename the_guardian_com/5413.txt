Three men have been shot and a fourth stabbed in late-night violence in part of Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, which has been troubled by rivalry between drug gangs.One victim is in hospital in a serious condition and police are reinforcing patrols in the Sheepridge area, where another man, 33, was shot and wounded last week. Forensic experts sealed off two streets, but Chief Inspector John Carter said there was no threat to public safety. The local councillor, Jean Calvert, and residents called for tougher action against drug dealers, warning that they were driving residents out.
