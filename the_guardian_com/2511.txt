Three men have denied shooting a five-year-old girl in a shop, as attempted murder charges were dropped against them.Thusha Kamaleswaran was left paralysed from the waist down after she was shot at the Stockwell Food and Wine store in south London in March.Nathaniel Grant, 20, Kazeem Kolawole, 19 and Anthony McCalla, 19, all from London, are due to go on trial in January.They each pleaded not guilty on Tuesday to causing grievous bodily harm with intent to Kamaleswaran and to Roshan Selvakumar, 35, who was also shot in the shop.They also denied the attempted murder of Shaun Bryan and having a firearm with intent to endanger life.But charges of attempted murder involving Kamaleswaran and Selvakumar were discontinued following a ruling by the judge.The men appeared via videolink at the Old Bailey and remain in custody.
