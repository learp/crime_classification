A newsreader, a hobo, a primary school teacher, a construction worker: Cate Blanchett plays all these characters and more in the idiosyncratic Manifesto, the first full trailer for which has just been released online.Manifesto: Cate Blanchett's multiple personalities for video artist – in pictures Manifesto is written and directed by German artist Julian Rosefeldt, and is due to receive its world premiere at the Sundance film festival later this month. Originally a multiscreen video installation at Melbourne’s Australian Centre for the Moving Image, but now reassembled as a 90-minute feature, Manifesto’s synopsis describes it as “an homage to the 20th century’s most impassioned artistic statements and innovators, from futurists and dadaists to pop art, fluxus, Lars von Trier and Jim Jarmusch ... this series of reenactments performed by Cate Blanchett explores these declarations’ performative components and political significance.”    Facebook    Twitter    Pinterest Blanchett reportedly plays 13 different characters in the full film, which could be seen as a progression from her role as one of multiple Bob Dylans in the Todd Haynes-directed I’m Not There.Manifesto screens on 23 January at the Sundance film festival.
