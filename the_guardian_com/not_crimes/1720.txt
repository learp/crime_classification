We’ve gone to town on this at the restaurant, using Taittinger Comtes de Champagne 2006 and Hennessy Paradis cognac, neither of which is cheap. Just apply good taste when picking substitutes. Serves one.1 cane sugar cube4 drops Angostura bitters15ml cognac5ml Grand Marnier100ml champagneOrange peel and gold leaf (optional), to garnishPut the sugar in a chilled flute glass, add the bitters and soak. Pour on the cognac and Grand Marnier, top with champagne, garnish, and sip away.• Ed Fitzpatrick is operations manager, Chewton Glen, New Milton, Hampshire
