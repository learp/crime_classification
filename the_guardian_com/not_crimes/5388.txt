A true umami dish, this hits flavour buds your guests won’t know they had. The asparagus and edamame salad also makes a great accompaniment to plain grilled steak, lamb or chicken.(Serves 4)For the marinade2 tbsp mirin2 tbsp soy sauce1 tsp brown sugar1 tsp sesame oil400g sirloin or rump steak, cut into 1–2cm slicesFor the dressing2 tsp wasabi paste1 tbsp soy sauce2 tbsp rice vinegar1 tbsp light olive oil1 tsp brown sugarFor the salad150g watercress or pea shoots200g asparagus, shaved150g cooked edamame beansBunch of spring onions, cut into strips1 tbsp black sesame seeds1 tbsp white sesame seedsMix together the mirin, soy sauce, brown sugar and sesame oil to make a marinade. Place the marinade in a resealable bag with the sliced steak and leave to marinate for an hour or overnight.Whisk together the dressing ingredients.Heat 1 tbsp rapeseed oil in a large pan or wok until hot, then carefully lift the steak slices out of the marinade, add to the pan and stir-fry quickly for a minute. Remove from the pan and leave to rest for 5 minutes.Dress the watercress, asparagus and edamame beans with the dressing. Arrange on a serving plate with the steak and top with the spring onions and sesame seeds.Leon: Happy Salads by Jane Baxter and John Vincent (Mitchell Beazley, £15.99).
