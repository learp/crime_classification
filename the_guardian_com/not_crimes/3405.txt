BBC spy thriller The Night Manager easily saw off the challenge of ITV drama Doctor Thorne, touted as the new Downton Abbey, to win the battle for the 9pm Sunday night slot.The £20m adaptation of John Le Carré’s spy thriller, which stars Tom Hiddleston, Olivia Colman and Tom Hollander, drew an average of 6.2 million viewers.Viewing was down slightly on the previous week, however the BBC1 drama still drew 27.9% share of audience between 9pm and 10pm. It was easily enough to see off the debut of ITV’s Doctor Thorne, an adaptation of Anthony Trollope’s novel by Downton Abbey creator Julian Fellowes, which drew 3.8 million viewers.The drama, which also stars Hollander, pulled in a 17% share of total TV viewing between 9pm and 10pm.Sunday night also saw the final in the latest series of celebrity winter sports reality show The Jump, which has been dogged by controversy following a string of accidents and picked up an average of 1.8 million viewers.BBC2’s new drama series Thirteen, about a woman who escapes after being held captive in a cellar for 13 years, premiered with an average of 900,000 viewers and a 5.8% share of total TV audience between 10pm and 11pm.
