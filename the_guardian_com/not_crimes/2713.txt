The Eurovision song contest will be broadcast in the US for the first time later this month, its organizers announced on Monday. The European Broadcasting Union said that it had reached a deal with Logo, a cable network best known for LGBT-themed programming that is part of Viacom group. As tradition demands, Zelmerlow will host this year’s semifinals and final competition, which will be held in Stockholm, Sweden. The contest is currently in its 61st year. This year’s theme is “Come Together”. In 2015, 197 million people tuned in to watch the final round. About 44.8% of them were 15 to 24 years old. Last year’s contest was hosted by Eurovision’s 2014 winner, Conchita Wurst, Austrian artist and drag queen. Since her victory in 2014, Wurst has become an icon and a spokesperson for an LGBT community. “It’s just unbelievable that nearly every gay human being knows who I am now – that’s overwhelming,” she told the Guardian after her win. According to EBU, the most successful Eurovision song contest winner was the Swedish pop band Abba, which won in 1974.
