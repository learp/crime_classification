Top Gear presenter Chris Harris has landed his own international show on the BBC just days after his co-presenter Chris Evans quit the BBC2 motoring series.Harris, a motoring journalist and presenter who was one of Evans’ first hires when he took over Top Gear from Jeremy Clarkson, will front a new show called Chris Harris on Cars, based on his popular YouTube series.It will air on BBC America in the US and on the BBC Brit channel – launched on the back of the success of Top Gear – in Australia, south America, South Africa, Australia, Poland and Scandinavia.Chris Evans quits BBC Top Gear after just one series The seven-episode series debuts in the US on 11 July.Harris was regarded as one of the successors of the rebooted Top Gear, which struggled in the ratings and suffered a mauling from critics.The BBC America show will see Harris get behind the wheel of the “newest, fastest and coolest cars on the road”, the broadcaster said.There is no suggestion the new show will affect his role on Top Gear, other than potentially make him more valuable as it attempts to appeal to an overseas audience.Top Gear is one of the BBC’s most valuable properties, and was last year worth £50m a year to its commercial arm, BBC Worldwide.
