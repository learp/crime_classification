Rogue One: A Star Wars Story has opened in China to significantly smaller box-office figures than its franchise predecessor, The Force Awakens.Rogue One: A Star Wars Story review – a sleek addition to the fleet According to figures published by Chinese research agency Entbot, Rogue One made a total of $30.6m on its first weekend, a drop of 57% from Force Awakens’ opening result of $53.6m, which was released in a near-identical calendar slot in 2016.A number of factors are thought to be at play: not only has the Chinese box office stagnated in 2016, with growth falling well short of expectations, but the Star Wars series has less traction in the country than in other areas of the world (the figures for Force Awakens were themselves considered somewhat disappointing). Rogue One’s run in cinemas – likely to be the standard four weeks given to overseas films – will be hampered even further by the arrival of Passengers, the Jennifer Lawrence/Chris Pratt space romance, released in China on 13 January.Record-breaking levels of pollution have also been cited as a reason that Chinese audiences may be staying at home.
