Born in the US Virgin Islands, Kelsey Grammer, 61, grew up in New Jersey and Florida. He became famous for his Emmy-award-winning portrayal of Dr Frasier Crane in the sitcoms Cheers and Frasier. Grammer voices Sideshow Bob in The Simpsons and his latest movie is Breaking The Bank.He is married for the fourth time and has six children. He divides his time between LA and New York.When were you happiest?This morning, surrounded by my children and my wife, Kayte.What is your greatest fear?Losing my mind.What is your earliest memory?A woman holding a banana out to me on a plane. I was two.Which living person do you most admire, and why?Putin. Because he is so comfortably who he is.What was your most embarrassing moment?In camp when I was eight: a bunch of kids threw me outside naked. I got ’em back!What would your super power be?Kindness.What is your favourite smell?Sunrise mist in the Catskills: it’s the scent of freshness, a little like the English countryside.What is your favourite word?Remember.Which book changed your life?A Passage To India by EM Forster. It opened my mind to a world I’d never known before.What did you want to be when you were growing up?Everything, which is probably why I ended up being an actor.What is the worst thing anyone’s said to you?When I was six, a girl said, “You’re ugly.” It’s stayed with me.What do you most dislike about your appearance?I have a gut I just hate.What is your guiltiest pleasure?I love to sit down and have my drink in the afternoon.
