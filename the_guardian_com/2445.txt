An unemployed labourer from Sunderland is expected to go on trial in February accused of hoaxing the Yorkshire Ripper inquiry with the notorious "I'm Jack" tape and three letters to detectives 27 years ago.John Samuel Humble, 49, of the Ford estate in Sunderland, was given the provisional date yesterday when he appeared via a video link from Leeds prison at the city's crown court.A request for bail was refused by the Recorder of Leeds, Judge Norman Jones, and Humble was remanded in custody, accused of perverting the course of justice, until January 9.
