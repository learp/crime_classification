A 70-year-old man has been charged with the murder of a teenage girl whose body was found in allotments 46 years ago.West Midlands police believe Jacqueline Marie Thomas, 15, was sexually assaulted and strangled to death. Her body was discovered in Bordesley Green, Birmingham in the summer of 1961.The man, who has not been named, appeared at Birmingham magistrates court earlier today.Jacqueline vanished after visiting a funfair with friends on the night of August 18, 1961. A week later her body was found at a disused allotment near her home. The teenager was one of eight sisters and the discovery of her body sparked a major police hunt. Posters of Jacqueline were plastered across Birmingham and hundreds of officers were involved in the search.
