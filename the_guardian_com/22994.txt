The husband of Nisha Patel-Nasri has been charged with her murder, Scotland Yard said last night.Fadi Nasri, 33, who was arrested on Tuesday, was charged with the special constable's murder, along with Roger Lesley, 37, who was first arrested last year.Four people have now been charged with the murder of Mrs Patel-Nasri's, who was found dead outside her home in Wembley, north London, last May. The others are Tony Emmanuel, 41, and Jason Jones, 35. They are next due at the Old Bailey on March 16.Mrs Patel-Nasri, 29, had been stabbed with a cook's knife. It is still unclear whether she had gone outside in her nightdress to investigate a disturbance, or whether she found someone in her house.The knife, which was missing from a block set in her kitchen after the attack, was later recovered by police and found to carry not only Mrs Patel-Nasri's DNA, but also that of her suspected attacker.Mrs Patel-Nasri was a hairdresser who performed unpaid police duties in her spare time. She had been a special constable for more than three years when she was killed.She and her husband had been celebrating their third wedding anniversary on the night of the attack.Mr Nasri and Mr Lesley will appear before west London magistrates court today.
