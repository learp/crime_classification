The death of a former Manchester Evening News journalist is being examined by police investigating the sabotage of saline drips at Stepping Hill hospital in Cheshire.Bill Dickson, the paper's news editor in the 1970s and 80s, died on 31 December, aged 82.He became the fourth person to die from the poisoning of saline drip while being treated at the Stockport hospital last summer.News of his death emerged following the arrest of a 46-year-old male nurse at the hospital.Dickson, a Glaswegian, retired from journalism in 1991 after two lengthy stints on the Manchester Evening News. His youngest son, Drew, now works at the MEN.Sources: Daily Telegraph/Manchester Evening News/HoldTheFrontPage
