Detectives continued to hunt yesterday for the attacker who stabbed a pregnant mother in the neck in front of her child, leaving her paralysed.Villagers in Little Bookham, Surrey have been warned to stay vigilant after Abigail Witchalls, 26, was attacked on Wednesday afternoon. She had gone out for a walk with her 21-month-old son Joseph who was in a buggy and was later found in a secluded lane. Mrs Witchalls has been left paralysed by the attack and fears have been expressed for her unborn child. Her condition was said to be stable yesterday Prayers were said for Ms Witchalls yesterday in Our Lady of Sorrows church in Effingham, Surrey, which the family attended. Surrey police said there had been 250 calls from the public following appeals for information. A 28-year-old man was held for more than 30 hours and questioned on suspicion of attempted murder but was released on bail on Saturday pending further inquiries. A 29-year-old woman arrested with him was eliminated from the inquiry and released without charge. The pair had voluntarily gone to Leatherhead police station after an appeal over a blue Peugeot seen near the scene of the attack. Police may try to interview Mrs Witchalls even though her ability to communicate is restricted to blinks and facial expressions. Her father, Martin Hollins, paid tribute to her yesterday saying: "She's making a tremendous fight of this."
