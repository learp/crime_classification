A Blackburn Rovers player will appear in court after allegedly making a homophobic gesture to the crowd while playing against one of his former clubs.Colin Kazim-Richards, 26, has been summonsed to appear in court to answer a charge under Section 5 of the Public Order Act, Sussex Police said.It is alleged that the forward made a homophobic gesture to the crowd during the Championship match against Brighton & Hove Albion at the Amex Stadium on 12 February.Kazim-Richards will appear at Brighton magistrates court on 22 August.
