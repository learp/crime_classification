A former Law Society president has been charged with making a forged copy of a passport. Robert Sayer, 54, allegedly sent an email under a false name to the solicitors' disciplinary tribunal about a colleague.He is then alleged to have submitted a copy of a Dutch passport in the false name, Thomas Fernando, to the tribunal. Mr Sayer, who faces four charges under the 1981 Forgery and Counterfeiting Act, was not present for the preliminary hearing at Bow Street court in London yesterday. The case was adjourned by District Judge Timothy Workman until May 25.
