A 17-year-old girl from London has appeared in court accused of plotting with a group of youths to lure a teenage boy to his death.The girl, from Hackney, who cannot be named for legal reasons, appeared before the Old Bailey charged with the murder of Jeremie Malenge, also 17.The victim had travelled from Swindon to Hackney in east London, where he was allegedly chased down and attacked by a group of youths late on 6 January.A postmortem examination carried out at Poplar mortuary found the cause of death was a stab wound to the chest.Scotland Yard said on Tuesday night that two boys – a 14-year-old from Hackney and a 17-year-old from Redbridge, north-east London – have also been charged with murder. They were due to appear in custody at Stratford youth court.Meanwhile, a 17-year-old boy arrested in West Yorkshire on suspicion of murder remains in police custody.Another youth, from Enfield, north London, who was also arrested on suspicion of murder, has been bailed pending further inquiries.It is understood that Jeremie was questioned on the night he was killed by officers because he matched the description of youths reported to be armed with knives in the area.He was not arrested but was attacked and killed less than an hour later in Ponsford Street, where emergency services pronounced him dead.The Independent Police Complaints Commission is investigating the Metropolitan police’s prior contact with the victim.Judge Timothy Pontius remanded the girl in custody until a plea and case management hearing at the Old Bailey on 1 April.
