Merseyside police yesterday arrested a man on suspicion of murder following a "horrendous" attack in which a 26-year-old man was stabbed four times in the Liverpool suburb of Mossley Hill.Detectives confirmed a 25-year-old man was being questioned about the murder in Mossley Hill on Monday afternoon. They said the killing was not connected with road rage. Witnesses said the dead man was stabbed following a row. The killer drove off in his car and the victim was later found slumped on the pavement. He was taken to hospital where he died about two hours later. The victim will not be identified until a postmortem examination has been completed. "This is a horrendous incident which has shocked this respectable area," said Supt Graham Yip of Merseyside police.
