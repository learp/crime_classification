March 31 2001: Stuart Lubbock, a 31-year-old father of two, is found dead in the swimming pool of entertainer Michael Barrymore's Essex home after a party. Mr Barrymore is questioned by police.October 2001: Barrymore accepts a caution for drugs offences and allowing his home to be used for smoking cannabis.September 2002: An inquest records an open verdict on Mr Lubbock's death. Medical experts tell the hearing he had suffered severe internal injuries, suggesting he may have been the victim of a serious sexual assault. Alcohol, cocaine and ecstasy were also found in his system.Mr Barrymore gives evidence but refuses to answer questions over allegations that he gave Mr Lubbock cocaine only hours before his body was found.September 21 2002: ITV announces that it has "no plans" to commission any further shows from Mr Barrymore, who had been one of television's highest-paid entertainers and was reportedly on a £2m contract with the channel.September 30 2002: Mr Barrymore tells GMTV he is "sad and sorry" over Mr Lubbock's death, and offers to meet his family.September 2003: The entertainer abandons a comeback attempt after the first three nights of his one-man show in London receive bad reviews.December 2003: Amid reports that he has received a £1.4m tax bill, Barrymore leaves the UK to live in New Zealand with his partner, Shaun Davis.January 2006: Mr Barrymore returns to UK screens as a Celebrity Big Brother housemate. He remains in the house for three weeks and finishes runner-up in the final poll, prompting speculation that his showbusiness career could be revived.After emerging from the house, he holds a tearful two-hour meeting with Mr Lubbock's father, Terry, in a London hotel. Terry Lubbock reportedly tells the entertainer he does not hold him "responsible" for what happened to his son. Mr Barrymore is said to have responded: "It haunts me every day".February 2006: Mr Lubbock's family are blocked from launching a private prosecution against Mr Barrymore. A district judge in Essex rules there is insufficient evidence for a hearing on six offences - four related to the misuse of drugs, one alleging drunk and disorderly behaviour and one alleging assault.April 19 2006: Police announce that they are reviewing Mr Lubbock's death as part of "routine" procedure.December 2006: Essex police begin a new inquiry into Mr Lubbock's death. Detectives say their decision to reinvestigate follows a review of the initial inquiry.January 2007: The Independent Police Complaints Commission says it has decided to investigate aspects of the original inquiry into Mr Lubbock's death after receiving complaints from his family.March 30 2007: On the sixth anniversary of Mr Lubbock's death, detectives investigating the case make a "direct appeal" to "those who have any information" about how he died.June 14 2007: Three men are arrested in connection with Mr Lubbock's death.September 10 2007: Mr Barrymore attends Harlow police station to answer bail.
