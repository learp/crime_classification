The number of killings in England and Wales has fallen by 17% in a year, annual crime figures show.There were 136 fewer cases of murder, manslaughter and child killing in the 12 months to the end of March, according to Home Office statistics. Overall crime was down 5% and violent crime was 6% lower.There was an increase in burglaries, although at 1% this was lower than expected given the recession.
