Isle of Man businessman Trevor Baines, 69, started a six-year jail term today for laundering "dirty money" in a multimillion-pound Wall Street share scam. Baines, who was put at 349th on the Sunday Times Rich List last year, was convicted of money laundering and false accounting in a £111m securities con. People in the Douglas courtroom on the Isle of Man gasped when the sentence was read out. His wife, Wendy Nicolau de Almeida Baines, 50, was found guilt of the latter charge.The "pump and dump" con used false accounting and bogus market reports to inflate share values.Baines made his £130m fortune working for the Africa House Group, his trading and private banking operation run from the couple's £2.75m home.
