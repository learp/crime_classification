A dissident republican man has been charged with the murder of a prison officer in Northern Ireland.The 33-year-old is due to appear in court on Wednesday in Craigavon magistrates court charged with killing David Black who died in an ambush on the M1 motorway in November 2012.The victim had been on his way to work at Maghaberry top security prison outside Belfast when he was shot dead.The 52-year-old father of two was the first member of the Northern Ireland prison service to be murdered by paramilitary groups in the region for more than 20 years.The suspect is also charged with possessing a gun with intent to endanger life, according to the Police Service of Northern Ireland (PSNI).On Tuesday night the PSNI thanked their counterparts in the Irish Republic for their help with the murder investigation.David Black was the 31st member of the prison service to be murdered during 40 years of conflict.A PSNI spokesperson said on Tuesday night: "Detectives from serious crime branch would like to thank An Garda Síochána for their help and support throughout this difficult and complex investigation which spanned both jurisdictions."
