A vicar was stabbed in the stomach as he walked to visit a parishioner in Stanmore, north-west London, police said yesterday. The Rev Ian Brady, in his 40s, was attacked soon after leaving the vicarage of St Anselm's church on Tuesday night.He managed to stumble the 100 yards to the parishioner's house, where he was found at the door with the knife still in his stomach. He was taken to hospital by ambulance.Mr Brady, who is married with two children, is in a serious but stable condition at Northwick Park hospital. Police have yet to establish a motive for the attack.
