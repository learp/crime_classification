A 33-year-old man appeared in court today charged with the double murder of French students Gabriel Ferez and Laurent Bonomo in south-east London last week.Nigel Farmer, unemployed and of no fixed address, is accused of killing the PhD students, both 23.The young men were found bound and stabbed to death in the bedsit they rented in New Cross. Farmer was also accused of arson and attempting to pervert the course of justice when he appeared before Greenwich magistrates court. He was remanded in custody until October 16 when he will appear at the Old Bailey.Last night police made two further arrests in connection with the murders.A 35-year-old man and a 25-year-old woman are being questioned today after they were detained at an address in Streatham.
