A teenager accused of murdering the Liverpool schoolboy Rhys Jones can be named today after he turned 18.Sean Mercer is no longer legally granted anonymity as he is not a juvenile.Rhys Jones, 11, was shot while walking home across the car park of the Fir Tree pub in Croxteth after playing football.Mercer, who was 16 at the time of the killing, is set to appear in court in Liverpool next month.Other youths have been charged in connection with Rhys's death, but Mercer is the only one accused of murder.James Yates, 20, of Croxteth, has been charged with possession of a prohibited firearm and of assisting an offender. Melvin Coy, 24, of West Derby, and two boys aged 16 and 17, who cannot be identified, are all charged with assisting an offender.Another 17-year-old boy has been charged with assisting an offender and possessing a firearm and ammunition.Mercer's mother, Janette, 48, has been charged with perverting the course of justice alongside James Hughes, 22, and Yates's parents, Francis Yates, 48, and Marie Yates, 50, all of Croxteth.
