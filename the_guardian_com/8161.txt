The Portuguese lorry driver involved in the M6 crash that killed a family of six has been charged with causing death by dangerous driving, Cheshire police said.Paulo Jorge Nogueira da Silva, 46, was today charged with six counts of causing death by dangerous driving.David Statham, 38, his wife Michelle, 33, and children Reece, 13, Jay, nine, Mason, 20 months, and Ellouise, 10 months, were returning to their home in Llandudno on Monday night.Their car was crushed and incinerated in a collision involving three lorries and two cars on the northbound carriageway between junctions 16 and 17.
