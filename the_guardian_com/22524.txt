A man appeared in court on Monday charged with starting a fire that destroyed the Reeves furniture store in Croydon during the recent riots.Gordon Edward Thompson, 33, is accused of setting the premises ablaze on 8 August.He appeared at Croydon magistrates court accused of two counts of arson.He spoke only to confirm his name and address during the short hearing. He is accused of two counts of burglary in relation to allegedly stealing a laptop, as well as various items from a branch of House of Fraser.Thompson is also charged with violent disorder in Croydon town centre. He was remanded in custody to appear at inner London crown court on 22 August.The Reeves store was set up in 1867 and its current owner Trevor Reeves is the fifth generation of the family to run the business. Reeves Corner in Croydon is named after the firm.
