Seven young men and two youths today admitted conspiracy to commit grievous bodily harm in connection with the murder of an army cadet.Joseph Lappin, 16, was visiting a youth club in Liverpool when he was unwittingly caught up in gang rivalry and stabbed through the heart.James Moore, 17, admitted murder at Liverpool crown court on Wednesday. Lappin was a cadet member of the Liverpool Scottish Regiment. His friend Callum Naden was stabbed twice, but survived.The nine changed their pleas and admitted being part of the gang that attacked the boys.Mr Justice Henriques told the nine they would all receive jail terms, adding: "What you all did was quite shocking. Regrettably, you will pay the price in due course." He remanded them in custody for sentencing.
