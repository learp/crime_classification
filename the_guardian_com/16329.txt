A 16-year-old boy was stabbed to death at a half-term party, allegedly after a drunken row with a friend.Police were called to a home in the Ovenden area of Halifax, West Yorks, late Friday night and the teenager was taken to Calderdale Royal Infirmary. He was pronounced dead on arrival.A 15-year-old boy was later arrested at home. Neighbours reportedly described the boys as 'close friends'.The teenagers reportedly attended St Catherine's Catholic High School. A schoolfriend, who did not wish to be named, said the victim and three other boys had been at the home of one of their elder sisters. 'I know them really well and have never known of them ever carrying knives,' she said.
