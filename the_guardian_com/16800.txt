A 16-year-old boy has been charged with the murder of Shane Jackson, 15, who was stabbed to death in Newcastle yesterday.The victim's body was discovered by police at a council flat in the west end of the city. He was the second teenager in two weeks to have been fatally stabbed in the run-down district.A Northumbria police spokesman said: "A 16-year-old youth has been charged with the murder of a 15-year-old boy at a flat in Hallow Drive, Throckley, yesterday."The boy who died can now be formally named as Shane Jackson, who was 15 and from Hallow Drive."The youth charged with murder has been remanded in custody to appear before Newcastle magistrates tomorrow morning."A 17-year-old boy and a man have been arrested in connection with assisting an offender.Police have also revealed results of a post-mortem examination, which showed Shane died from multiple stab wounds.Tributes have been placed on the popular Gone Too Soon website.One entry read: "Shane was easy-going who loved to have a laugh. He cared dearly about his family and his two dogs. He always was a bit of a handful but never meant any serious harm."He loved his basketball and put a smile on many faces, sadly not everyone got to see the best in Shane."Detectives stressed Shane's death was not being connected with the alleged murder of 16-year-old Mark Smith in the Elswick area of Newcastle, on May 3.On Guardian UnlimitedMore on crimeUseful linkNorthumbria police
