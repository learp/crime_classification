A homeless man was tonight charged with the murder of two French students whose charred bodies were discovered in a London flat.Unemployed Nigel Edward Farmer, 33, of no fixed abode, was charged with killing the two PhD students, Gabriel Ferez and Laurent Bonomo, on June 29, Scotland Yard said. He was also charged with one count of arson and one count of attempting to pervert the course of justice.He was arrested early on Monday after walking into Lewisham police station, south-east London. He will appear at Greenwich magistrates court in the morning.The bodies of Ferez and Bonomo, both 23, were found in a bedsit in New Cross, south-east London, on June 29. They had been tied up and repeatedly stabbed in the head, neck, and torso before the bedsit was set alight.Bonomo was stabbed 196 times and Ferez 47 times.
