A teenager has appeared in court charged with the murder of a young mother found dead in a car.In a 30-second hearing at Thames magistrates court, Myles Fraser Williams, 19, spoke only to confirm his name, address and date of birth. He is accused of killing 20-year-old Kirsty Treloar and will appear at the Old Bailey on Friday. A provisional plea and case management hearing was set for 15 March.Williams, from Hackney, east London, is also charged with the attempted murder of Treloar's brother Dean, 22, and sister Gemma, 24.Treloar's body was discovered with stab wounds on Monday in a grey Fiat Stilo, which was abandoned about a mile from her home in Hackney. She was pronounced dead at the scene.The woman's siblings were treated for stab wounds in hospital on Monday. A month-old girl was also taken to hospital as a precaution.
