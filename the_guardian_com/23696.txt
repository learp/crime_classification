A man has been arrested in connection with an alleged assault on former Celtic captain Neil Lennon. Now a coach at the club, Lennon was treated for minor injuries after an incident on Glasgow's Ashton Lane on September 1.Strathclyde police confirmed they are holding a 46-year-old man in custody, who is expected to appear in court tomorrow. Police said the arrest came as a "direct result" of the video footage and still images they released shortly after the attack. They are still trying to trace a second man alleged to have been involved in the incident and are appealing for witnesses.The incident happened after Celtic's 4-2 defeat at home to Rangers. "The assault came after Neil was subjected to sectarian abuse," said police at the time.
