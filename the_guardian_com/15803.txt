Four men charged with murdering a private detective more than 20 years ago, including his business partner, were remanded in custody today.A fifth man, a former Metropolitan police detective sergeant, has been charged with conspiracy to pervert the course of justice in connection with the killing of Daniel Morgan, 37, who was found with an axe in his head in the car park of the Golden Lion pub in Sydenham, south London, in March 1987.Morgan's business partner, William John Rees, 53, of Weybridge, Surrey, was last night charged with murder along with Glen Vian, 49, of South Croydon, Surrey. Earlier in the day, James Cook, 53, a builder from Tadworth, Surrey, and Vian's brother Garry, 47, unemployed and of no fixed abode, were charged with murder.All have been remanded in custody to appear at the Old Bailey on July 31.The former police detective is Sidney Alexander Fillery, 61, now a barman from Great Yarmouth, Norfolk. He was attached to Catford police station in south London when Morgan was killed.A sixth man, a serving Metropolitan police officer, has been suspended and arrested on suspicion of misconduct in a public office. He is on bail to appear in court on June 2.The six were arrested on Monday after the fifth inquiry into the murder.
