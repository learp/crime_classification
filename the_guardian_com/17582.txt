Three women were arrested and later bailed today by detectives investigating the alleged abduction of Shannon Matthews, the nine-year-old who went missing for almost a month in Dewsbury, West Yorkshire.Amanda Hyett, 25, her sister Caroline Meehan, 29, and their mother Alice Meehan, 49, were held on suspicion of perverting the course of justice. All three were questioned at police stations in West Yorkshire, before being released on bail pending further inquiries. Hyett is the neighbour of Shannon's mother Karen Matthews and the sister of Matthew's partner, Craig Meehan.Hyett and Alice Meehan were arrested and bailed last week in connection with the girl's disappearance.The further interviews come a day after Karen Matthews appeared in court charged with perverting the course of justice and child neglect.
