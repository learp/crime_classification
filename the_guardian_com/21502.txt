Bafta-winning comedy actor Chris Langham has withdrawn from BBC projects after he was charged with eight counts of indecent assault and one other sexual offence.In a statement released through the BBC, the star of BBC4 satire The Thick of It said: "In view of all the circumstances, I have decided to withdraw from all projects with the BBC until these matters are resolved."Langham was charged when he answered bail at a police station in Kent today. He was previously charged with making indecent images of children.He was bailed to appear before magistrates in Sevenoaks, Kent, tomorrow afternoon.A Kent police spokesman said: "The Crown Prosecution Service has advised Kent police to charge Christopher Langham, a 57-year-old actor from the Cranbrook area [of Kent] with eight counts of indecent assault and one other sexual offence."Officers charged Mr Langham under the Sexual Offences Act of 1956 when he answered bail at a police station in Kent today. He was bailed to appear to appear before Sevenoaks magistrates tomorrow afternoon."In a statement released today following the latest charges, Langham said he was determined to clear his name."I have today been charged with further offences by Kent police," he said. "I have nothing further to add to what I have said previously, namely that I am determined to clear my name but cannot comment further on the allegations due to legal proceedings."I request once again that the privacy of my family is respected and thank the media for their restraint in this regard so far."The actor said he would not be making any further comment.Langham, who is married with five children, was charged in May with 15 counts of making indecent images of children.The future of the hit BBC4 show is likely to be dependent on the outcome of the court case involving its lead actor.
