Hundreds of Hell's Angels formed a mile-long funeral cortege through London yesterday in memory of Gerry Tobin, who was shot dead on the M40 last month.More than 1,500 motorcyclists from around the world gathered in east London before heading across the city to Mortlake Crematorium. Many wore red and white badges with the words 'Gentleman Gerry'.The 35-year-old mechanic from Mottingham, southeast London, was killed on 12 August as he returned from the Bulldog Bash bikers' festival at Long Marston, near Stratford-upon-Avon. Three men have been charged with murder and a fourth with a firearms offence.
