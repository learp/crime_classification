A man accused of murdering policeman Ian Broadhurst in Leeds on Boxing Day was today remanded in custody until February 18.David Bieber, who is also known as Nathan Wayne Coleman, appeared before Leeds Crown court. During a ten-minute hearing, he was also charged with attempting to murder PC Broadhurst's colleagues PC Neil Roper, 45, and 26-year-old PC James Banks.PC Broadhurst, 34, was shot during a routine traffic arrest in the Oakwood area of Leeds by a man in a stolen BMW.Mr Bieber was arrested at a Gateshead hotel on New Year's Eve after an appeal for information by West Yorkshire police resulted in more than 300 phone calls. US-born Mr Bieber, 35, made no application for bail, according to the Press Association.
