A 14-year-old boy was today charged with the rape of four primary school girls.The teenager - who has not been named - was charged with seven counts of rape following sex attacks on two eight-year-old girls, a seven-year-old girl and a 10-year-old girl in a Greater Manchester park on Sunday. The girls had been playing on swings in Mandley Park, Salford, shortly before the attack took place between 5pm and 6.40pm. A boy approached them and took them to a secluded area of the park, where he raped them.Earlier in the week, the park was cordoned off while specialist forensic officers searched for clues.The boy, from Salford, was arrested by detectives at around 3.25am on Monday."A 14-year-old boy from Salford has today been charged with seven counts of rape on four children," a Greater Manchester police spokeswoman said. "The charges follow an incident in Mandley Park on Sunday July 10." The boy is due to appear before Salford magistrates court tomorrow.
