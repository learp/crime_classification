Police charged a man with attempted murder today after shots were allegedly fired at two police officers. Callum Hall, 20, was charged with two counts of attempted murder, possession of a firearm with intent to endanger life and possession of a weapon in public. The incident happened between Waterloo railway station and Southwark tube station in south London on 5 August. One of the officers sustained a minor injury. The two uniformed constables were on patrol in a marked car when they encountered the suspect. Hall will appear before Tower Bridge magistrates court tomorrow.
