A homeless man was last night charged with the murder of two French students whose charred bodies were discovered in a flat in south-east London.Nigel Farmer, 33, unemployed, of no fixed abode, was charged with killing PhD students Gabriel Ferez and Laurent Bonomo on June 29, Scotland Yard said. He was also charged with one count of arson and a count of attempting to pervert the course of justice.He was arrested early on Monday after walking into Lewisham police station. He will appear at Greenwich magistrates court today.Last night a 35-year-old man and a 25-year-old woman were also arrested in connection with the killing. The bodies of Ferez and Bonomo, both 23, were found in a bedsit in New Cross, south-east London, on June 29. They had been tied up and repeatedly stabbed in the head, neck and torso before the bedsit was set alight. Bonomo had been stabbed 196 times and Ferez 47 times.
