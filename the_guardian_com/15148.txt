A 21-year-old man was stabbed to death in Liverpool yesterday in what detectives said was a racially motivated attack.The man, of mixed race, was stabbed in Garston, in the south of the city. Officers described it as a "targeted attack". Two white teenagers, aged 17 and 16, were arrested at the scene on suspicion of murder. Merseyside police are looking for another 17-year-old from Garston and a further youth. Officers were called to Byron Street at around 1pm yesterday following reports of a fight. The victim, believed to be from the area, was taken to the Royal Liverpool University Hospital, where he died at around 3pm.A post-mortem examination established that he died from a stab wound to his side.
