A 57-year-old man has been arrested in connection with the attempted murder of police officers in north Belfast.Detectives detained the man on Monday in relation to an incident at Brompton Park in the Ardoyne area of the city late on 12 July when a number of shots were fired at police lines following rioting.At least 10 shots were fired after a gunman emerged from a group of rioters who had been attacking the police for several hours with bricks, stones, petrol bombs and fireworks.Police deployed riot squad officers and three mobile water cannon to quell the violence.The violence erupted at the sectarian interface close to the Crumlin Road following the most controversial Orange Order parade in Northern Ireland's loyalist marching season.No one was injured in the shooting but the Police Service of Northern Ireland said it was treating the incident as the attempted murder of police officers.
