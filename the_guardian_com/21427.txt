Police have recovered more than £2m of counterfeit cash after officers investigating a multimillion pound jewellery fraud arrested four men in Manchester on Sunday.Greater Manchester police said the men were stopped at the Portland Thistle hotel in the centre of the city shortly after 2pm.The men were in possession of more than £2m worth of counterfeit euros, about £50,000 worth of legitimate euros and more than £100,000 of high value watches. They were arrested on suspicion of fraud and taken into police custody for questioning.DI Rob Cousen, of the force’s serious and organised crime group, said: “This type of crime causes a lot of harm to legitimate businesses and I hope the action this weekend reassures local businesses and residents we are committed to tackling organised crime in all its guises.”Anyone who believes they have been a victim of fraud was asked to contact police on 101.
