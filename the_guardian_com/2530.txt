A man aged 54 will appear in court in Derry today charged with the murder of a police officer in 1981.Father-of-two Reserve Constable John Proctor, 25, was gunned down by the IRA outside the hospital in Magherafelt, Co Derry, after visiting his wife, who had just given birth; she was waving to him from a window when he was ambushed.Hours earlier, Proctor had been a pallbearer at the funeral of a UDR soldier friend, who was also gunned down two days previously. The 54-year-old was arrested in the village of Swatragh, south Derry, yesterday; he is also charged with possessing a firearm with intent.
