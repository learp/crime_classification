New measures to tackle harmful drinking by girls and their involvement in low-level violent crime are to be explored as part of the updated youth crime action plan. Research is also to be undertaken into how girls are now being drawn into gang culture.The revised action plan published today acknowledges recent interest in the issue of violent crime committed by girls but stresses that boys still commit the overwhelming majority of crime, including violent crime.A national "girls get moving" day is planned for this summer to improve their participation in the national sports and leisure social inclusion programme, Positive Futures.Schools are also to be asked to ensure that their alcohol education work is tailored to girls. A general summer anti- alcohol campaign is to run over the next couple of months.
