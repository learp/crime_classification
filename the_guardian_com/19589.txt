Four teenagers aged from 15 to 19 were being questioned today in connection with the suspicious death of a 15-year-old girl whose body was found in a field.The girl's body was discovered in the Allerton area of Bradford, West Yorkshire, late last night, reportedly by a member of the public. Police said postmortem tests had so far failed to establish a cause of death. A West Yorkshire Police spokeswoman said four local teenagers were arrested this morning in connection with the incident: a 15-year-old boy and three girls, aged 17, 18 and 19. She said all four were in custody for questioning.The spokeswoman said that no charges could be brought until the cause of death was established. More details of the circumstances surrounding the girl's death will be released later today. A police spokesman said: "Police in Bradford are investigating the death of a 15-year-old girl who was found in a field adjacent to Canford Drive, Allerton, Bradford, last night. The death is being treated as suspicious but no further details are available at present. Anyone with details should contact Bradford North police."Police received a call from a member of the public at around 8.45pm last night and the girl's body was discovered by officers shortly afterwards. Detectives are believed to be investigating claims that the teenager may have been involved in a fight shortly before her death.A force spokeswoman said the girl had not been reported missing.
