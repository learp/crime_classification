A man who was arrested in connection with the murder of a Catholic police officer in Northern Ireland will be charged with a number of firearms offences this weekend.The man will appear at Dungannon court on Saturday. He is not charged with murder.The 33-year-old, from Omagh, has been charged with possession of explosives, firearms and articles likely to be of use to terrorists.Two other men arrested over the murder of Constable Ronan Kerr were released "unconditionally" on Tuesday.Kerr was killed when a bomb exploded under his vehicle in Omagh earlier this month.
