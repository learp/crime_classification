Seven people appeared in court today in connection with the murder of the 11-year-old Liverpool schoolboy Rhys Jones.Rhys was shot dead in Croxteth last August as he walked home from football practice.A 17-year-old boy accused of murdering the schoolboy appeared via video link alongside six others at Liverpool crown court. None of the defendants entered a plea.They were all remanded in custody to appear in court on June 30. The Recorder of Liverpool, Henry Globe QC, told the defendants their trial was likely to begin on October 2.James Yates, 20, of Croxteth, Gary Kays, 25, and Melvin Coy, 24, both of West Derby, Liverpool, as well as two boys aged 16 and 17 who cannot be identified, are all charged with assisting an offender.A second 16-year-old is charged with assisting an offender and possessing a firearm and ammunition.The arrests followed one of the biggest murder inquiries carried out by Merseyside police in recent years.Rhys, who had been shot in the neck, died in his mother Melanie's arms in the car park of the Fir Tree pub.Thousands attended his funeral in Liverpool's Anglican cathedral.
