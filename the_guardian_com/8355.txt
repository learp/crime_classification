The Serious and Organised Crime Agency says it has identified 130 criminal masterminds whom it will treat as priority targets.Soca, Britain's version of the FBI, has been told by the prime minister to "make life hell" for criminal gangs and is prioritising drugs and human trafficking.Its director general, Bill Hughes, said the target list was drawn from more than 1,000 people considered "players at a serious level" in crime.Mr Hughes said Soca, which has been low-profile since it began work in April this year, has more than 250 active operations and has made more than 200 arrests.
