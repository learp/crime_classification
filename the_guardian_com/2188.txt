An 18-year-old man has appeared in court charged with murdering a mother and her three teenage children, who died in a house fire a week ago.Kemo Anthony Porter, who is accused of killing Shehnila Taufiq, 47, her daughter Zainab, 19, and sons Bilal, 17, and Jamil, 15, was not required to indicate any plea during a two-minute hearing at Leicester magistrates court.Porter, of Leicester, spoke only to confirm his name, address and date of birth, and was remanded in custody to appear by videolink at the city's crown court on Monday.Meanwhile, inquests were opened at Leicester town hall into the deaths of the family at their home in Wood Hill, Leicester.The inquest heard Taufiq was a mature student living in Leicester with her children, who were also studying in the city.Temporary Detective Superintendent Kate Meynell, senior investigating officer at Leicestershire police, told the hearing the four family members had been formally identified by a dental examination. The cause of death was given as smoke inhalation.Leicester coroner Catherine Mason adjourned the inquest to a date to be set.No family were at Friday's hearing.In a statement issued on Thursday, Assistant Chief Constable Roger Bannister of Leicestershire police said there was no suspicion of a racially motivated element to either the house fire or, separately, the fatal stabbing elsewhere in the city of 20-year-old Antoin Akpom.
