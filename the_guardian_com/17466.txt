Ships should be turned into prisons in a bid to ease the overcrowding crisis gripping Britain's jails, according to the Home Office. The news comes as 19 police forces are holding prisoners in cells, an emergency measure known as Operation Safeguard.The jail population reached 79,714 on Friday - a level still 65 below the all-time high of two weeks ago.Now it has emerged that the Home Office is advertising in the Official Journal of the European Union asking firms to provide 200 to 800 prison places on ships.'The Home Secretary has said he wants us to explore innovative solutions to the prison capacity issue,' a Home Office spokeswoman said.Juliet Lyon, director of the watchdog Prison Reform Trust, accused the Home Secretary, John Reid, of acting in 'desperation'.
