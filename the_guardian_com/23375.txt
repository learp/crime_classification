A 17-year-old boy who died after he was stabbed in the chest in Bolton has been named as Cody Turner.He died after a disturbance at a house in Tonge Moor last night. Turner was found collapsed in the road with a stab injury to his chest at around 11.30pm. It is believed the injury was sustained during an incident at a nearby house. He was taken to Royal Bolton hospital where he died.A postmortem is due later today. Officers are advising motorists to avoid the area because road closures are in place while detectives deal with the scene.A 17-year-old boy from the area has been arrested on suspicion of murder and is being questioned by police. He has not been charged with any offence.Two 18-year-old men and a 34-year-old woman were also arrested by Greater Manchester police on suspicion of assisting an offender.A neighbour, who lives on the same road as the murder scene, said the teenager was "a lovely lad". The woman, who did not want to be named, said: "My daughter knew Cody really well. They used to hang around together a lot. She has been sent home from school because she is so upset."Shop supervisor Mark Latham, 33, said the Tonge Moor community was very close."It is really tight-knit. I live with my partner just up the road and I heard about it shortly after it happened. I was really shocked, everyone I have spoken to is. When you kick one of us down, you get all of us really."A young mother who had just dropped her child at Castle Hill primary school, in the road behind the murder scene, said the incident had "really shaken" her."It is scary knowing you are leaving your children in an area where this happened," she said. "You get a lot of kids hanging around, drinking in the evenings, but I've never heard anything like this before."
