Four teenagers were arrested yesterday by detectives investigating the murder of Ben Bellamy, 17, who was beaten to death, stripped and dumped in the sea at Mumbles, near Swansea, early on Sunday.Two youths aged 15 and two aged 17 held by police in dawn raids are believed to be among a group of seven youths seen with Ben shortly before he died.Ben's uncle, Tony Rees, 44, appealed for witnesses to come forward, saying: "He was an extremely intelligent boy looking forward to going to university. His mother, father and especially his younger brother Karl are devastated."
