Two people from Derby have been arrested following a fatal house fire in which six children from the same family died.A 34-year-old man and a 29-year-old woman have been arrested in connection with the inquiry following the fire in Victory Road that killed the children, aged between five and 13. Sources said the pair had not previously been subject to police scrutiny. They have not been identified.The house fire, which broke out in the early hours of 11 May, killed Jade Philpott, 10, and her brothers John, nine, Jack, eight, Jesse, six, and Jayden, five. Their older brother Duwayne, 13, died in hospital two days later.The children's parents, Mick Philpott, 55, and his 31-year-old wife, Mairead, have been charged with six counts of murder and have been remanded in custody. During a preliminary hearing at Nottingham crown court, the couple were told they would go on trial on 14 January next year.Their next court appearance is on 5 November for a plea and case management hearing. The children's funerals took place in their absence.Assistant Chief Constable Steve Cotterill, of Derbyshire police, said: "These latest arrests are part of the ongoing investigation into circumstances surrounding the fire. Despite the fact that two people have already been charged with murder, we are continuing our investigations to ensure that all lines of inquiry are completed. No further information will be released at this time."Separately, in June, a 45-year-old man was arrested in Derby in connection with the investigation. He is due to answer police bail at a later date, but detectives refused to say what charge he was questioned about.In the immediate aftermath of the fire, a 28-year-old woman and a 38-year-old man were arrested but then released without charge.
